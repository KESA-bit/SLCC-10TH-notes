Social Science I - Chapter 1: Humanism

PART 1: Renaissance & Humanism

1. What are the characteristic features of the changes that took place in Europe from the 14th Century onwards?
• Spread of secular values • Growth of urban life • Monetary economy • Progress of trade • Importance gained by regional languages • Humanistic perspective (Humanism).

2. The Renaissance in Europe first began in Italian cities. Give reasons?
• They were more independent, wealthy, and dynamic.
• Traders in these cities were well aware about banking and insurance.

PART 2: Renaissance Art & Literature

3. What were the features of Renaissance Paintings?
• Realistic depiction of the human body.
• The use of colour to illustrate light and spatial depth.
• Use of oil paint, portraits, and landscapes.

4. Name the famous Renaissance writers and their regional works:
• Dante: The Divine Comedy (Italian)
• Petrarch: Sonnets addressing Laura (Italian)

PART 3: Science & Reformation (Final Part)

5. Discuss the contribution made by Renaissance science to astronomy?
• Nicolaus Copernicus: Argued the centre of the universe is not the Earth, but the Sun (Heliocentric model).
• Galileo Galilei: Developed the telescope and proved heliocentric theory.
