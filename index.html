Social Science I - Chapter 1: Humanism

PART 1: Renaissance & Humanism

1. What are the characteristic features of the changes that took place in Europe from the 14th Century onwards?
• Spread of secular values • Growth of urban life • Monetary economy • Progress of trade • Importance gained by regional languages • Humanistic perspective (Humanism).

2. The Renaissance in Europe first began in Italian cities. Give reasons?
• They were more independent, wealthy, and dynamic.
• Traders in these cities were well aware about banking and insurance.
• Some of them had expertise in technical matters of trade, such as bookkeeping.
• Many of them had become extremely wealthy through trade.

3. Compare Scholasticism and Humanism:
• Scholasticism: Deeply rooted in Christian theology, prioritised the divine and the afterlife.
• Humanism: Emphasis on human experience, worldly life, rational inquiry, emotions, and capabilities.

PART 2: Renaissance Art & Literature

4. What were the features of Renaissance Paintings?
• Realistic depiction of the human body.
• The use of colour to illustrate light and spatial depth.
• Use of oil paint, portraits, and landscapes.

5. Match the Famous Artists and Works:
• Leonardo da Vinci: Last Supper, Mona Lisa
• Michelangelo: Ceiling of Sistine Chapel, Last Judgment (Paintings) / Pieta, David (Sculptures)
• Donatello: Bronze sculpture 'David', Gattamelata

6. Name the famous Renaissance writers and their regional works:
• Dante: The Divine Comedy (Italian)
• Petrarch: Sonnets addressing Laura (Italian)
• Boccaccio: Decameron (Italian)
• Niccolò Machiavelli: The Prince (Italian)
• Erasmus: In Praise of Folly (Netherlands)

PART 3: Science & Reformation (Final Part)

7. Discuss the contribution made by Renaissance science to astronomy?
• Nicolaus Copernicus: Challenged the geocentric model and argued that the Sun is the centre of the universe (Heliocentric model).
• Galileo Galilei: Developed the telescope, challenged geocentric beliefs, and proved heliocentric theory.

8. What marked the beginning of the Protestant Reformation?
• Martin Luther, a priest, pasted his famous 'Ninety-Five Theses' on the doors of the Wittenberg Cathedral protesting against the Church's sale of indulgences.
